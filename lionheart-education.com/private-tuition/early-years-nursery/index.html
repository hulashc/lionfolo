Early Years

Pre-School Years & Nursery

We believe early education should be joyful, playful, and pressure-free. Before age four, most learning happens through curiosity — exploring, imagining, and making sense of the world in their own way. Our early years programmes embrace this philosophy, while also supporting families with expert advice on nursery and school choices.

Why play matters…

The science behind early learning

With 80% of brain development completed by age three, the early years are when children’s minds are most receptive to learning. Research from Harvard shows that guided play can be more effective than direct instruction for developing mathematical and spatial skills in young children.

When children play, their brains release chemicals that support memory, attention, and social skills. The parts of the brain most developed in early years are those that respond to active experiences – not passive instruction.

Our Services

Supporting early years development

While we don’t recommend formal tutoring at this stage, families preparing for prep school or 4+ assessments can benefit from warm, informal sessions with our specialist Early Years tutors.

  • Play-based learning

    Informal sessions led by early years specialists that nurture curiosity, communication, and creativity. Ideal for families who want to gently introduce learning without compromising on fun.

  • School entrance preparation

    Age-appropriate support to build confidence ahead of 4+ assessments — including early number sense, phonics awareness, turn-taking, and expressive language.

  • Reception readiness

    Light-touch tuition for children starting school soon, with a focus on early literacy, number play, classroom routines, and self-expression.

  • Homeschooling programmes

    For families who prefer to delay formal schooling or follow a home-led path, we offer structured early learning at home — part-time or full-time — with tutors who can also provide nanny-style care.

Frequently asked questions

  • How can a private tutor help my child at this age?

    At this stage, a private tutor creates rich learning experiences through play, exploration, and gentle guidance. Rather than formal lessons, they focus on building confidence, curiosity, and foundational skills like language development, early numeracy concepts, and social interaction. Our tutors help children develop the joy of discovery that becomes the foundation for all future learning.

  • Is my child too young for private tuition?

    We believe children are never too young to benefit from quality educational support, but the approach must be completely age-appropriate. Before age 4, we focus on play-based learning rather than formal instruction. If your child shows curiosity, enjoys stories, or asks lots of questions, they’re ready to benefit from gentle, nurturing educational experiences.

  • How does private tuition work at this age?

    Sessions are entirely play-based and child-led, lasting 45-60 minutes maximum. Our tutors bring carefully selected materials – books, games, art supplies, building blocks – that encourage natural learning through exploration. Activities might include storytelling, singing, creative play, or nature exploration, all designed to feel like wonderful playtime.

  • When should I start preparing for 4+ assessments?

    Gentle preparation can begin around 18 months before the assessment through natural play activities that develop the skills schools look for – listening, following instructions, social interaction, and basic cognitive abilities. However, we strongly advise against formal preparation before age 3, as this can create anxiety and resistance to learning.

  • How do you select tutors for early years children?

    Our Early Years tutors undergo our most rigorous selection process. Beyond academic qualifications, we look for individuals with specific early childhood experience, natural warmth with young children, and understanding of child development. All undergo enhanced DBS checks, and we observe them interacting with children before selection.

  • Where is tuition held?

    Early Years tuition works best in familiar environments, so we typically come to your home where your child feels most comfortable. We can also provide tuition in gardens, local parks, or child-friendly venues. The key is creating a relaxed, pressure-free environment where natural learning can flourish.

  • What's the difference between nursery learning and private tuition?

    While nurseries focus on group activities and social development, private tuition offers completely individualised attention tailored to your child’s interests, learning style, and pace. Our tutors can follow your child’s curiosity in the moment – spending longer on activities they love or gently introducing new concepts when they’re most receptive.

  • How long should sessions be for this age group?

    Sessions typically last 45-60 minutes, but we’re completely flexible based on your child’s attention span and engagement. Some 2-year-olds thrive with 30-minute sessions, while engaged 4-year-olds might enjoy longer periods. Our tutors are skilled at reading children’s cues and ending on a positive note.

  • Should I be thinking about school admissions?

    If you’re considering independent schools with 4+ entry, it’s worth understanding the timeline and requirements early. However, the best preparation at this age is simply nurturing your child’s natural curiosity, confidence, and love of learning. Schools look for happy, engaged children who can separate from parents and interact positively with others – qualities that develop naturally through quality play-based experiences.
